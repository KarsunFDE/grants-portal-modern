Workflow:
1. Intake
2. Screening
3. Peer Review
4. Award

HITL Gates:

We use human approval at key risk points.

Gate 1: Eligibility and Risk Review
	Stage: Screening
	Owner: Grants Officer or Program Officer
	Input: Application data, eligibility checks, risk flags, AI recommendation, retrieved_sources, citation_refs, confidence_score, grounding_status
	Allowed decisions:
		- Approve to move forward
		- Return for fixes
		- Reject
 - Rule: No application can move past Screening without a human decision and rationale.
 
Gate 2: Conflict of Interest (COI)
	Stage: Peer Review
	Owner: Review Lead
	Input: Reviewer assignments, COI flags, reviewer disclosures
	Allowed decisions:
		- Resolve and continue
		- Remove reviewer and reassign
		- Override with written rationale
 - Rule: No scoring starts until COI is resolved by a human.
 
Gate 3: Factor Suggest Acceptance
	Stage: Peer Review
	Owner: Human reviewer
	Input: AI factor suggestion output, retrieved_sources, citation_refs, confidence_score, grounding_status
	Allowed decisions:
		- Accept
		- Edit
		- Reject
 - Rule: AI suggestions are assistive only and require human acceptance before use in scoring narrative.
 
Gate 4: Award Decision
	Stage: Award
	Owner: Grants Officer
	Input: Peer review results, recommendation package, AI-assisted summaries, retrieved_sources, citation_refs, confidence_score, grounding_status
	Allowed decisions:
		- Award
		- Do not award
		- Return to review
- Rule: No unattended award path exists. Award must have a persisted human approval record and rationale.

Grounding Policy:
For regulatory or policy answers:

- AI must reference real sources: 2 CFR 200, 45 CFR 75, and NOFO content.
- If output is low-confidence, missing citations, or ungrounded:
	- Block output
	- Escalate to human review through a named gate
- Rule: Ungrounded regulatory guidance is never directly shipped.

Grounding Escalation Routing:
- Screening grounding failures route to Gate 1 owner.
- COI-related retrieval issues route to Gate 2 owner.
- Factor suggestion grounding failures route to Gate 3 owner.
- Award package grounding failures route to Gate 4 owner.
- Rule: No silent retries for blocked grounding cases. Every escalation creates a gate decision record with gate_id and owner.

Retrieval Invalidation Policy:
Re-retrieval is required before the next gate decision when any of the following change:
- Application data
- NOFO or amendment content
- Policy corpus version
- Reviewer assignments or COI state
- Award package content
- Rule: Prior evidence cannot be reused after invalidation.

Tenant Binding Invariant:
- Every evidence_ref must carry the same authority-derived tenant_id as the gate decision tenant_id.
- Rule: A gate decision is invalid if any evidence_ref tenant_id differs from decision tenant_id.

Cache Revalidation Policy:
- Cache hits are allowed only if they pass citation, confidence, freshness, and tenant checks immediately before generation and gate input.
- Rule: If any cache check fails, block cached output and escalate to the gate owner.

Audit and Durability Requirements:
Every gate decision must be logged in an append-only trail:

 - actor_id
 - actor_role
 - tenant_id
 - timestamp
 - workflow_stage
 - gate_id
 - ai_run_id
 - decision
 - rationale
 - override_flag
 - evidence_refs
 - retrieved_sources
 - citation_refs
 - confidence_score
 - grounding_status

System requirements:

 - Logs survive restart
 - Logs support multi-day pause and resume
 - OIG can reconstruct decisions from logs alone
 - No cross-tenant data leakage
 
Correct Regulatory Mapping
 - 2 CFR 200.205 = merit review
 - 2 CFR 200.206 = risk review
 
Acceptance Criteria
1. Award cannot be completed without human approval record.
2. Screening cannot advance without Gate 1 decision.
3. Peer review scoring is blocked until COI gate is resolved.
4. Factor Suggest output cannot be used until human acceptance.
5. Ungrounded regulatory output is blocked and escalated.
6. Audit trail captures all required gate fields.
7. Audit trail is durable across restart and pause.
8. Tenant boundaries are enforced in logs and gate evidence
