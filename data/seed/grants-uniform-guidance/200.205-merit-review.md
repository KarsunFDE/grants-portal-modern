# § 200.205 — Review of Merit of Proposals (starter stub)

Unless prohibited by Federal statute, prior to making a Federal award the
Federal awarding agency must design and execute a merit-review process for
applications. The merit-review process must be described or incorporated by
reference in the applicable notice of funding opportunity (see § 200.204 and
Appendix I).

Merit review is a thorough, consistent, and objective examination of
applications based on pre-established criteria (commonly: significance of the
project, soundness of the approach/work plan, and feasibility, including the
qualifications of the principal investigator/project director). Panels of peer
reviewers score applications against the published criteria and document the
basis for their recommendations.
