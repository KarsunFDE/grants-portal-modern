# § 200.204 — Notices of Funding Opportunity (starter stub)

For competitive grants and cooperative agreements, the Federal awarding agency
must announce specific funding opportunities by publishing a notice of funding
opportunity (NOFO). To give applicants sufficient time to prepare applications,
the awarding agency should make the NOFO available for at least 60 calendar
days; in no event should the period be less than 30 calendar days unless
exigent circumstances require otherwise.

The NOFO must include, at minimum: the Assistance Listing Number and title;
the funding opportunity description; award information (estimated total program
funding, anticipated number of awards, and funding instrument type — grant or
cooperative agreement); eligibility information; application and submission
information; the merit-review criteria the agency will apply; and Federal award
administration information (Appendix I to Part 200 specifies the full content).
